Genesis 12:1-3; 15; 17 - God's covenant with Abraham is unconditional and everlasting. It was sealed by God alone (Gen 15:17), not dependent on human obedience.

Key Truth: The Mosaic Law (given 430 years later, Gal 3:17) did not nullify the Abrahamic Covenant. The Law was added to reveal sin and point to the Messiah, but the promise to Abraham‚Äîland, offspring, and blessing to all nations‚Äîstands forever.

Romans 11:29 - "For the gifts and the calling of God are irrevocable." God's covenant with the physical descendants of Abraham (the Jewish/Hebrew people) remains in force.

The 144,000 (Revelation 7:4-8; 14:1-5): These are literal physical descendants from the 12 tribes of Israel‚Äîsealed servants during the tribulation. They are not the Church (which includes believing Gentiles grafted in, Rom 11:17-24), but rather Jewish believers who fulfill God's enduring promise to preserve and redeem Israel.

Gentiles & the Covenant: Believing Gentiles are "grafted in" (Rom 11) and become Abraham's spiritual seed through faith (Gal 3:29), sharing in the blessings. But this does not replace Israel's unique role and future restoration (Rom 11:25-27; Jer 31:31-37).
